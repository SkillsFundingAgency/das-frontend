We need YOU!

Express your interest in shaping the apprenticeship service.

For employers that do not pay the apprenticeship levy who want to take part in testing the apprenticeship service. Apply between now and 30 June 2019.

The apprenticeship service is for organisations that want to take on apprentices.

Users can:

  • Set up and manage apprenticeship training
  • Get funding for apprenticeship training
  • Create and upload apprenticeship job adverts

The service is currently only open to employers who pay the apprenticeship levy. We are planning to open it up to all employers in 2020 and would like the help of businesses who do not pay the apprenticeship levy to help us test the service to ensure that it works for everyone.

How will you be helping us?

We’re expanding this service to include those businesses who do not pay the apprenticeship levy and who are looking to take on apprentices.By signing up to take part you will be able to help us learn, shape and develop the new digital Apprenticeship Service.

Why should I sign my business up to do this now?

By being part of the digital service from the start, as well as shaping the service for future users, you will get hands on help and support as you progress along your journey to hiring and managing new apprentices.

Types of employers we need help from...

We need help from different types of small and medium sized businesses who plan to have apprentices starting apprenticeships this Autumn (August, September or October).

Apprentices can either be existing employees, who are ready to start an apprenticeship, or new apprentices you will need to recruit.

What would be involved in the process?

If you would like to express an interest in helping, check your eligibility below and follow the link to register. If you are selected to take part you will get early access to our new digital service and we will support you through the process of registration, creation and management of your new apprentices.

All we really need from you is a willingness to take part and to give us feedback on how you find the process of using our new online digital service for apprenticeships.

Application criteria...

Employers and training providers must have already agreed the apprenticeship before they apply.

They must have a plan and agreement for an apprentice start, for a specific standard and in a specific month. Both the employer and the training provider must agree who will submit the application and become the lead contact?

Providers must be government-approved

At the point of the EOI application submission, the named training provider must be on the Register of Apprenticeship Training Providers, with an existing contract that allows them to work with employers who do not pay the apprenticeship levy and must also have money left in their current allocation for non-levy funding.

This is for small and medium-sized businesses only

You cannot take part in this testing if your organisation pays the apprenticeship levy. Register for an apprenticeship service account if you pay the levy.
